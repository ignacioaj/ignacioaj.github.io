Short Bio

I’m a Biomedical Engineer fascinated by the human body – from molecules to complex organ systems – and by the ways technology can help us understand and improve it. I enjoy solving problems with code, whether analyzing medical data, processing images, or exploring AI applications in healthcare.

I graduated in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Málaga, balancing strong academics with hands-on experiences that shaped my professional path.

During an Erasmus stay in Finland, I had the chance to work with EEG and ECG signals for pathology detection, which sparked my interest in signal processing. In my bachelor’s thesis, I applied AI techniques to identify radiation-induced chromosomal abnormalities in micrographs.

I want to keep growing in the medical technology field—especially in medical data science, imaging, and AI-driven solutions—because that’s the kind of contribution I want to make to the world: using technology to genuinely improve people’s care and well-being.
